# Revised Slide Deck — FairShare Commons
**Built on the existing `fairshare-commons.pptx` (10 slides). Keep the design/layout; swap the content below.**

> What changed vs. the current deck: (1) reframed around **refugee energy** (Amara/Kakuma/EfR), (2) added the **AI co-pilot** as a core beat, (3) added the **RLO side** so it's two-sided, (4) added the **closed loop** and a **business model** line. The equity spine and visuals stay.

Design notes: keep text minimal, one idea per slide. Use dignified visuals — solar panels, a map pin on Kakuma, abstract scales — **never a child's face** (EfR judges will read that as extractive).

## Slide 1 — Title  *(keep, minor tweak)*
- **FairShare Commons**
- Subtitle: *Balancing the scales of community support*
- One line under it: *Fair discovery for refugee-led organizations and the donors who want to fund them.*
- Team: Alex · Antonio · Caroline · Polderr × Energy for Refugees

## Slide 2 — The Problem  *(reframe to refugees/energy)*
- Header: **THE PROBLEM**
- Big line: *Funding follows networks, not need.*
- Body: *Amara runs a solar project for 200 children in Kakuma refugee camp. Her work is excellent — but no donor has ever found her. Every year, refugee-led organizations doing the hardest work stay invisible, while a handful of large, well-connected names absorb most of the funding.*

## Slide 3 — Why it happens  *(keep the 3 biases; add a 4th that sets up the AI)*
- Header: **WHY IT HAPPENS** — *Donors want to help, but the tools guide them down a narrow path.*
- **V — Visibility Bias:** Search rewards organizations with big tech budgets, not local impact.
- **O — Outreach Disparity:** Large institutions out-shout small, grassroots efforts.
- **F — The Familiarity Loop:** We give to the names we already recognize.
- **L — The Language Barrier** *(NEW):* Grassroots leaders can't pitch in the polished "language of money" donors expect — so even great work gets passed over.

## Slide 4 — The Solution  *(expand: matching engine + AI co-pilot)*
- Header: **THE SOLUTION** — *An equity-first matching engine, plus an AI co-pilot that removes the writing barrier.*
- Body: *FairShare Commons connects intentional donors with the refugee-led and grassroots organizations that need them most — and helps those organizations get discovered in the first place. Two halves of one solution: fair matching, and a co-pilot that turns rough field notes into a fundable profile.*

## Slide 5 — How it works  *(keep the 3 principles — they're our actual features)*
- Header: **HOW IT WORKS** — *Three design principles to rebalance visibility.*
- **01 — Prioritizing the Underserved:** Results run from least- to most-funded. Our equity layer deliberately lifts the organizations with the fewest resources. *(this is our anti-Matthew engine)*
- **02 — Unbiased Discovery:** Initial results hide logos and names. You connect with the mission first, not the marketing budget. *(blind search)*
- **03 — Shared Contributions:** Split one donation across a circle of organizations, weighted toward those with the biggest funding gap. *(portfolio)*

## Slide 6 — The AI Co-Pilot  *(REPLACES the old "Cooperative Demonstration" — this is the live-demo anchor)*
- Header: **THE AI CO-PILOT** — *From a WhatsApp note to a fundable project.*
- Three steps (visual):
  1. **Write** — rough notes, in any language, even over WhatsApp from a basic phone.
  2. **Polish** — AI produces a professional, donor-ready profile in the donor's language. It keeps real numbers; it invents nothing.
  3. **Publish** — the profile becomes a live project, instantly discoverable by matched donors.
- Footer: *This is where we'll show you live.* ← **trigger the live demo here**

## Slide 7 — Two-sided journey  *(expand donor-only into both sides)*
- Header: **HOW EVERYONE WINS** — *A genuine reason to show up, on both sides.*
- **Donor:** Select causes → discover (least-funded first, names hidden) → build a giving circle → split one contribution → sustain.
- **Refugee-led org:** Write rough notes → AI polishes → publish a project → get discovered by fit → find and request aligned donors.
- Caption: *Both sides reach out. No one is a service provider to the other.*

## Slide 8 — Why it matters  *(strengthen with accessibility + equity language)*
- Header: **WHY IT MATTERS** — *A structural shift toward fair access.*
- Body: *Grassroots and refugee-led initiatives are embedded in their communities but depend on fragile, network-bound funding. We lower the barrier to near zero: a low-threshold, any-language, WhatsApp-ready intake — and an equity-by-design engine that refuses to privilege the already-visible. The burden sits with the side that has the most capacity, not the least.*

## Slide 9 — Our invitation + how it sustains  *(keep invitation; add the business model)*
- Header: **OUR INVITATION** — *Build this with us.*
- **Grassroots & RLO partners:** Help us connect more underserved, regional initiatives with global support.
- **Pilot supporters:** Route your next giving circle through this framework.
- **How it sustains** *(NEW line):* *Free for refugee-led orgs and individual donors. Funded by a small fee on institutional and corporate flows, plus foundation grants — never charged to those with the least capacity.*

## Slide 10 — Conclusion  *(keep, tie to the mission)*
- Header: **CONCLUSION**
- *FairShare Commons helps donors give well — and helps the most overlooked organizations be seen.*
- *For donors: clarity. For the organizations doing the hardest work: a fair share.*
- **Thank you.**
