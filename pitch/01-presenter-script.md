# Presenter Script — FairShare Commons
**~3 minutes spoken + 90-second live demo · 3 presenters**

> Practice with a timer. Target 3:00–3:30 total. The demo is the heart — protect its 90 seconds.

## Who says what (assign by strength, not by who built what)
- **Presenter A — The Problem** → put your *strongest storyteller* here. The opening lands the EfR judges.
- **Presenter B — The Live Demo** → the person most comfortable driving the app live (recommended: whoever knows the build best, so they can recover if something hiccups).
- **Presenter C — The Principle + Close** → confident closer; also quarterbacks Q&A.

All three field questions; Presenter C directs traffic.

---

## PRESENTER A — The Problem (~50s) · Slides 1–3

**(Slide 1 — title)**
"Hi, we're [team names], and we built **FairShare Commons** — solidarity infrastructure for refugee-led organizations and the donors who want to fund them."

**(Slide 2 — Amara)**
"Meet Amara. She runs a solar project for 200 children in Kakuma refugee camp. Her work is excellent. But she has never received a Western donor grant — not because she's unworthy, but because **no one outside her network knows she exists**, and she can't write a proposal in the language donors expect."

**(Slide 3 — the number)**
"And she's not alone. Every year, **$31.3 billion** flows through the humanitarian system. **Less than 1.2%** reaches local organizations like hers. The rest flows through the same networks, to the same big names, every year. This isn't a money problem — it's a **discovery problem**."

*Handoff:* "So we asked: what if the right fit could surface automatically — without needing the right connections? [Presenter B] will show you."

---

## PRESENTER B — The Live Demo (~90s) · Slides 4–6 (advance quickly through 4–5, demo on 6)

> Drive slowly. Narrate every click. If it breaks, switch to the backup recording (see runbook) — never debug live.

"This is FairShare Commons. Two sides: organizations and donors. Watch both.

**First, I'm Amara.** I don't speak the donor's language, so I write in mine." *(paste the Swahili note)* "Just a few rough words about my project." *(click **Polish with AI**)*

*(3–5s)* "In seconds, our AI turns that into a **professional, donor-ready profile** — problem, beneficiaries, budget, SDG tags — **in the donor's language**. Notice two things: it kept my real numbers, and it **invented nothing**. Now I publish it." *(tick solar, amount 18000, location 'Kakuma, Kenya', **Publish as project**)*

"That project **did not exist a moment ago**. Now switch to the donor side." *(switch to donor tab)* "A donor cares about solar in Kenya. They declare what they care about **before they see any names** — so big brands get no head start." *(click **Find projects**)*

"And here's Amara's project — a **Strong fit**. And notice it's ranked **above better-funded organizations**: our equity layer deliberately lifts the under-funded, so the orgs who need it most aren't buried. Found **by fit — not by who she knows**."

*Handoff:* "[Presenter C] — why this is fair, and how it survives."

---

## PRESENTER C — Principle + Close (~50s) · Slides 7–10

**(Slide 5 — fair & accessible)**
"Three things make this work. **One — accessibility:** any language, and in production, over **WhatsApp** from a basic phone. The AI removes the writing barrier that keeps grassroots orgs out. **Two — equity by design:** our anti-Matthew layer corrects the bias that buries small orgs on every other platform. **Three — it's mutual:** donors and organizations both reach out. We deliberately rejected ratings, scores, and impact-per-euro. This is **solidarity, not a marketplace**."

**(Slide 6 — close)**
"It runs on one AI call and a simple backend — cheap enough for a small NGO coalition to operate. **Free for refugee-led orgs and individual donors**, funded by a small fee on institutional flows. No gatekeepers. No rating systems. Just a **fair path to discovery — for the organizations that need it most**. Thank you — we'd love your questions."

---

## Pacing cues
- A: don't rush the Amara line — let it land. Pause after "no one knows she exists."
- B: the magic beat is the polished profile appearing. Stop talking for one second and let them read it.
- C: hit "solidarity, not a marketplace" crisply — it's the line that wins the EfR judges.
